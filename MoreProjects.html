100 Men on a Mission:

Past Projects

100 Men on a Mission has gone beyond signing a check, we have gone to the community to make more change happen.

Socks of Love (December 2021)

Another year, another backseat FULL of socks that 100 Men On A Mission (with an assist from 100+ Women Who Care) donated to Socks of Love, solo run by Mick Grossman. Keep up the great work, Mick!

Dorothy's House (June 2021)

This small but mighty crew tackled two afternoons of hot sweaty work as we tackled some indoor projects and laid some decking for their second house. More tremendous fellowship and hard work. 100 Men (and women) doing their part for our amazing non-profit friends.

Socks of Love (November 2020)

Nice little 're-feet' performance by the men as we donated hundreds of pairs of new socks to Socks of Love and our good friend Mick Grossman. Gotta love a guy who has a personal mission to help keep people socked all year round. Keep up the good work Mick!

Encore Thrift Store (October 2020)

It wasn't the most pleasant weather but this hearty group tackled some landscaping and clean up work for our friends at The Encore Thrift Store. We followed up the hard work with some fellowship and fun conversation at The Hall and The Foundry so that's always a bonus! Looking forward to more projects to come.

Home Improvement For A Truly Inspirational Lady (August 2020)

Who hasn't had a small cut on the hand or finger or toe and thought nothing of it? That's exactly what Trisha thought back in January 2019. But a devastating bacteria infected the cut and made its way into Trisha's bloodstream. Serious complications arose that ultimately led to the amputation of both of Trisha's hands and feet ...

Now, 18 months later and with a heart to feed the hungry, Trisha, along with her friends and family, are embarking on a 5k walk with a goal of raising some needed funds for Meals From The Heartland. As part of getting to know Trisha's story in more detail, it became apparent that she could use some help with some home improvements. In stepped the 100 Men Project Team! Here is Trisha's take on some jobs well done. If you would like to learn more about Trisha's journey, please click here.

An awesome group of men swarmed upon my home for two Thursdays in a row, left all kinds of greatness in their wake, and stole all my cookies! O.K., I actually had to force the cookies on them because they were way too polite to take too many otherwise...

100 Men On A Mission is a group of men, some young and just starting out, some retired, some in the middle, but all with BIG hearts to assist the DSM community in various ways. As a group, they are able to do so much more than any of them could do on their own, and so their group was formed. Helping me out with all my handyman type tasks is case in point! I had a very large list of things needing done around my house. So, the offer from 100 Men On A Mission to help me out was such a blessing! Cole came both Thursdays as well and worked from my home while being here to answer questions they may have, and also finishing off my ramp.

Mike, whom my good friend, Al, has been working with from Meals From The Heartland for my walk, is part of this group. Al and Mike know each other from Kiwanis Miracle League. And Mike is also a former co-worker of mine back when we were both new to Norwest in the late 80's. Now sing "It's a small world after all..."! Al and his buddy Roger also came to help on the second Thursday.

The guys were INCREDIBLE! They accomplished tasks such as planting vines on trellises in my front yard, placing flower hangers in my bedroom and office, hanging a TV and ceiling fan, installing a Nest thermostat in my home, installing a Nest keypad on my front door, installing a remote keypad for my garage, hanging curtains in all rooms, assembling and installing a bookcase in my office and shelf in my bathroom, and fixing my kitchen sink, literally!

Thank you from the bottom of my heart to these individuals for helping out an old lady - Mike, Bryant, Bruce, Lee, JB, Caleb, Al, Roger, and Cole. And my gratitude goes out to 100 Men On A Mission as a group! God bless! Cookies anyone...anyone? Here's a sampling of their handywork!

International Relations Hard at Work! (June 2020)

We received an email from a concerned mom in Canada who leads a 100 Women's group there. Due to the COVID situation and travel restrictions, the mom and her family were not able to help her daughter move to Des Moines to start her 5-year residency. The daughter had never been to Des Moines so there was some obvious concern and apprehension from the mother's perspective.

Not to worry, a small but mighty team of 100 Men and family members met Sam and got her unloaded and moved in less than an hour! This is what Iowans do best - help others. Love the fact that we have a strong network of caring individuals associated with our 100 Men group. Rock on men, until the next project!!!

HAPPY FEET In The Works! (November 2019)

Oh my! Over 500 pairs of socks delivered in response to our collection effort. Fantastic work by both the 100 Men and 100 Women groups as part of their November meetings. If you Instagram then check out @socksoflove1 to see more about how this grass roots effort, led by Mick Grossman, is helping our neighbors in need. It warms the heart to work together to help our neighbors in need.

Dorothy's House - Chicken Coop and Run (April 2019)

This motley crew continued our project support at Dorothy's House by revamping their chicken coop and run. A great afternoon of fellowship and hard work. Two mighty men (Bruce and Michael) went back to do some finish work (roofing) and it's great to see men rally to support the great causes underway in our community. Keep up the great work men!

H.U.G.S. 2.0 (November 2018)

For the second year in a row, we collected "H.U.G.S." - Hats, Underwear, Gloves, and Socks and presented them to Bethel Mission as part of their winter drive. And this year, our friends at 100+ Women Who Care joined in and together we presented over 22 bags (literally hundreds and hundreds of items) for those in need this winter. We are without question, better together.

Dorothy's House Landscaping (October 2018)

A small but mighty group of 100 Men joined some women from 100+ Women Who Care to continue our relationship with Dorothy's House. We shoveled, wheel-barrowed, and distributed mulch throughout the property. The Pergola that we installed is still standing strong! Thanks to Michael Adams, Mike Schneider, Tom Fischer and Bruce Beckwith for spending some time on this project..

H.U.G.S. (November 2017)

During the winter months, some of the most needed items are "H.U.G.S." - Hats, Underwear, Gloves, and Socks! In response to this need, and in response to a call for action at our November meeting, 100 Men donated hundreds of items to help those in need. This collective gift was presented to Bethel Mission as part of their winter drive. Yet another way to answer the call right here in our community.

Dorothy's House Pergola (Summer 2017)

During our May 2017 meeting, Kellie Markey, the Executive Director of Dorothy's House, thanked the men for their generous gift from our February meeting. She was then asked if there was anything else she needed at the transitional house and her immediate response was "labor to install a pergola". 'Nuf said!'

A sub-group of 100 Men rallied to the cause and over the course of three days, the pergola became a reality. In addition to the labor, we were able to get the wood donated through some personal contacts (thanks to Reid and Wolf Construction)! All in all, a great way to expand the reach of 100 Men and pay it forward to our community partners who are doing great work here in central Iowa.
